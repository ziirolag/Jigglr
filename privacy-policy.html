Jigglr Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

Jigglr is a macOS menu bar utility that keeps your Mac awake by simulating small mouse movements and optional clicks. Your privacy matters. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

The short version

Jigglr does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal data. Everything the app does happens locally on your Mac. There are no analytics, no tracking, no accounts, and no servers operated by us that receive your information.

Information we do not collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or contact details.
  • We do not track your location.
  • We do not record your keystrokes, mouse activity, screen contents, or the apps you use.
  • We do not use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.
  • We do not create user accounts or profiles.

Information stored on your device

Your settings (such as movement interval, appearance, and feature toggles) are stored locally on your Mac using the standard macOS preferences system. If you unlock PRO with a redeem code, that code is stored locally on your device so the app remembers your PRO status. This information never leaves your Mac and is not accessible to us.

Accessibility permission

To simulate mouse movement and clicks, Jigglr asks for macOS Accessibility permission. This permission is used solely to move the cursor and, if you enable it, to click. Jigglr does not read, log, or transmit anything through this permission.

Purchases

Jigglr is free. The optional PRO upgrade is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store using In-App Purchase. We never see or receive your payment details. Apple's handling of purchase data is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

Children's privacy

Jigglr does not collect data from anyone, including children, and is not directed at children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
